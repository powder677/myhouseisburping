Why Does My Heating System Pop and Burp?

HVAC noises like popping, banging, or burping are primarily caused by thermal expansion of metal ducts, pressure imbalances ("oil-canning"), or clogged condensate lines. While often loud, most sounds are normal physical reactions to temperature changes, though persistent grinding or hissing requires professional attention.

Your Heating, Ventilation, and Air Conditioning (HVAC) system is the lungs of your home. As explained on our homepage, when you force hot air into cold metal, physics takes over. HVAC house noises are the most common source of complaints for homeowners.

Top 5 Reasons Your HVAC Makes Noises

  1. Thermal Shock (Duct Expansion): When a furnace blasts 120°F air into 60°F sheet metal ducts, the metal expands instantly, causing a loud pop.
  2. The "Oil-Canning" Effect: Undersized ducts or thin metal can be sucked inward when the blower starts, creating a booming sound.
  3. Condensate "Burping": AC units produce water. If the drain line is clogged, water gets trapped, creating a gurgling sound as air bubbles pass through.
  4. Dirty Filters (The Gasp): A clogged filter restricts airflow, causing a high-pitched whistle or a "gasp" when the cycle ends.
  5. Loose Brackets: Vibration can loosen straps, leading to rhythmic rattling.

Diagnosing the Sound: Burp, Bang, or Tick?

Translate your HVAC system's language:

Sound Likely Cause The Fix
Bubbling / Gurgling Clogged Condensate Drain Check drip pan & clear line
Loud Metallic Bang Thermal Duct Expansion Insulate ducts (or ignore if rare)
Repetitive Ticking Fan Blade / Bearing Issue Call a pro if it gets louder
Whistling Dirty Air Filter / Closed Vents Replace filter immediately

DIY HVAC Checks

Before calling for help, try these simple steps:

  • Replace your air filter immediately.
  • Ensure all supply vents are open to reduce pressure.
  • Listen near the furnace unit—if the sound is grinding or screeching, turn it off.

If the noise persists or you smell burning, read our guide on When to Call a Professional.

How to Stop House Burping?

To stop house burping noises, you must reduce friction and temperature shock. The most effective fixes are insulating metal HVAC ducts to muffle expansion pops, maintaining 40-50% indoor humidity to prevent wood shrinkage, and flushing your water heater to remove sediment causing "kettling."

Living in a noisy home can be stressful. While many sounds are physical inevitabilities, learning how to stop house burping involves addressing the root cause: friction, pressure, and air movement.

Top 5 Ways to Stop the Noise

  1. Insulate Metal Ducts: The loud "pop" from your HVAC is metal expanding. Wrapping ducts helps mitigate temperature shock, especially during cold weather snaps.
  2. Control Humidity: Dry wood shrinks and squeaks. Using a whole-home humidifier (or properly burping the house for ventilation) keeps wood swollen and tight.
  3. Fix the Water Heater: If your tank is popping, it's sediment. Learn how to flush the tank in our water heater noise guide.
  4. Secure Loose Pipes: Check where pipes run through floor joists. If they are loose, add plastic pipe clamps (not metal) to stop rattling.
  5. Clean Your Vents: A whistling return vent is often just dirty. If the whistling only happens during storms, check our wind noise solutions.

Fixing the Sound: Burping vs. Creaking vs. Popping

Different sounds require different tools.

Stopping "The Burp" (Plumbing)

The Fix: Clear the Vents. If your toilet burps, the roof vent might be clogged. Clearing this allows sewer gas to escape.

Stopping "The Pop" (Thermal)

The Fix: Lubrication & Loosening. Vinyl siding often pops if nailed too tightly. If the pop comes from the ceiling, it might be roof truss uplift.

Stopping "The Creak" (Floors)

The Fix: Talcum Powder. Sprinkle talcum powder into cracks to lubricate wood rubbing together.

When DIY Isn't Enough

You have insulated, lubricated, and tightened, but the noise persists. If the sounds are accompanied by cracks or leaks, do not keep trying to patch it yourself.

When Should I Call a Professional for House Noises?

You should call a professional if house noises are accompanied by physical symptoms. While popping sounds are often normal thermal expansion, call an expert if you see visible cracks in drywall, experience doors that stick, smell burning or gas, or spot water stains. These are signs of structural failure, electrical hazards, or leaks.

We have established that houses are noisy creatures. Most of the time, these sounds are benign conversations between wood, metal, and air. But sometimes, a house "burp" is actually a scream for help. Knowing when to call an expert can save you thousands of dollars.

Top 5 Warning Signs (When to Worry)

If your house is making noises and showing any of these symptoms, it is no longer a normal house sound. It is a structural concern.

  1. The "Gunshot" + Cracks: A loud bang is common in freezing cold weather. However, if it leaves a jagged crack in your drywall, you need to check if it's just roof truss uplift (cosmetic) or foundation failure (critical).
  2. The Hiss + Smell (Gas): If you hear a hissing or "burping" sound near a utility line and smell rotten eggs, leave the house immediately. This is a gas leak.
  3. The Sizzle (Electrical): A buzzing or sizzling sound behind a wall, often accompanied by a fishy or burning plastic smell, indicates arcing wires. This is a fire hazard.
  4. The Wet Thud: If you hear a dull thud or dripping sound in a ceiling, followed by a stain appearing, you have an active plumbing leak or roof failure. Ensure this isn't just condensation from poor ventilation.
  5. The Scrabble (Pests): Scratching, scampering, or chewing sounds are not thermal expansion. Review our guide on identifying pest noises versus settling before calling a builder.

Who to Call: A Routing Guide

"House burping" is vague. To get the right help, you need to match the sound to the correct trade. Use this table to decide who to contact.

The Sound Likely Issue Who to Call
Loud Popping / Banging Ductwork expansion or furnace issues HVAC Technician
Gurgling / Rumbling Plumbing vents or water heater sediment Licensed Plumber
Creaking + Cracks Foundation shifts or structural damage Structural Engineer
Sizzling / Buzzing Arcing wires (Fire Risk) Electrician (Emergency)
Scratching / Gnawing Mice, rats, or squirrels Exterminator

Is Your House Burping?

Don't Panic. Here is the complete guide to the pops, creaks, and groans your home makes.

Is It Normal?

"House Burping" is a term describing the sudden release of built-up pressure in a home. This pressure release manifests as loud pops, bangs, or cracks. The most common causes are thermal expansion (materials shrinking/growing with temperature), water heater sediment (kettling), or HVAC ductwork flexing when the heat turns on.

Just like a human burp is the release of gas, a house burp is the release of tension. While it can be startling, it is rarely a sign of imminent collapse. However, knowing the source of the sound is crucial for your peace of mind.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does my house pop at night?

This is thermal contraction. As the outside temperature drops after sunset, your siding, roof trusses, and window frames shrink. This movement creates friction that releases as a loud pop.

Is house burping dangerous?

In 90% of cases, no. However, if you see jagged cracks in walls, smell gas, or have doors that won't close, you should consult a structural engineer immediately.

How do I stop the noise?

You can't stop thermal expansion, but you can dampen it. Adding insulation, securing loose siding, flushing your water heater, and using flexible HVAC ducts can significantly reduce the volume.
